Artificer

The Artificers are ingenious inventors and tinkerers, driven by a relentless curiosity to explore the boundaries of science, engineering, and technology. They see the world not as a series of static objects but as a puzzle to be solved�a collection of systems waiting to be understood, improved, and perfected. Artificers stand as masters of innovation, blending arcane-like technology and mechanical wonders that mimic the abilities of magic, but without the need for spells or incantations.
At the core of the Artificer�s craft lies an unshakable belief in the power of invention. They wield tools, gadgets, and devices not as mere weapons, but as extensions of themselves and their understanding of the world. Their creations�a series of mechanical constructs, powerful cannons, and advanced armor�serve as both their means of survival and their greatest achievements. Every device is a testament to their skill, an elegant solution to a complex problem, and a unique fusion of precision engineering and cutting-edge design.
The origins of the Artificer�s craft are closely guarded by the gnomes, a reclusive people with a deep knowledge of the hidden arts of invention. For centuries, the gnomes have passed down their most treasured secrets, often in cryptic blueprints or obscure instructions, shrouding their advances in mystery. These secrets are said to be drawn from an ancient lineage of crafters�those who understood that the world could be reshaped and controlled through ingenuity alone. Their knowledge has allowed the gnomes to harness the very forces of nature, shaping them into tools and machines of unimaginable power.
Artificers are the ones who have taken these secrets to the next level, applying their mastery of craftsmanship and mechanics to build devices that can replicate the incredible feats of magic. Through careful tinkering and experimentation, an Artificer can construct anything from an indestructible suit of armor to a mechanical servant or even a cannon-like weapon that can unleash destruction with precision. These feats, while not magical in nature, are often perceived by those outside the world of invention as nothing short of magical.
In battle, Artificers are a unique force. They are not the towering warriors or spellcasters that many other adventurers are, but rather the creators of complex, often deadly devices that can change the tide of combat. Their gadgets�whether they are explosive traps, mechanical beasts, or specialized weapons�are designed to give them an edge in any situation. An Artificer�s true strength lies not in their physical prowess but in their ability to outthink their enemies and craft the right tool for any task.
At their core, Artificers are always tinkering, always looking for the next breakthrough. With each new invention, they unlock greater potential, finding new ways to harness the resources of the world and turn them into something extraordinary. Their work is not just about constructing machines, but about understanding the fundamental principles that govern the universe and using those principles to improve life for themselves and others.
The gnomes, in particular, are fiercely protective of their secrets. Their societies are built around a web of knowledge and tradition, passed down through generations in secrecy. Artificers often work in isolated workshops, deep in the heart of gnome strongholds, where their creations are nurtured and honed away from the prying eyes of those who would seek to exploit their knowledge.

Class Features
As an Artificer you gain these features. 

Infused Tools
1st-level Artificer feature
You gain the ability to imbue mundane objects with unique enhancements using your Crafting Points. Choose one type of artisan�s tools. You are always considered proficient with that tool and can use it to craft at double the normal speed.
You can spend 1 hour during a rest infusing a mundane item with specialized functionality by expending Crafting Points. The object gains one of the following benefits for 8 hours or until you use this feature again:
* A weapon grants an additional +1 bonus to attack rolls and damage (costs 2 Crafting Points).
* A suit of armor grants a +1 bonus to Dexterity saving throws (costs 2 Crafting Points).
* A set of tools grants a +2 bonus on one type of skill check related to that tool (chosen when infused) (costs 1 Crafting Point).
You can have only one item infused at a time. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). You regain all expended Crafting Points for this feature after a rest.

Crafting Reservoir
1st-level Artificer feature
You have a special reservoir of raw materials and energy that you can use to power your creations. This reservoir can store a limited amount of resources that you can spend to enhance or repair objects you craft.
You gain a Crafting Reservoir with a maximum capacity equal to twice your Intelligence modifier (minimum of 2). The reservoir refills after a rest. You can spend points from your Crafting Reservoir to craft enhanced gadgets, repair items, or infuse tools.
* Craft Enhanced Gadgets: Spend points to craft gadgets with additional features or enhancements, increasing their potency or effects.
* Repair Items: Spend 1 point to repair any item (weapon, armor, gadget, etc.), restoring 1d6 hit points per point spent.
* Infuse Tools: You can use Crafting Reservoir points in place of other resources to infuse mundane items with temporary enhancements (as per your Infused Tools feature), allowing you to extend its effects or activate stronger infusions.
When you expend points from the Crafting Reservoir, you cannot exceed your maximum capacity. The Crafting Reservoir does not refill during a short rest but will refill fully after a rest.

Field Mechanic
1st-level Artificer feature
Your understanding of devices allows you to make quick battlefield repairs. You can use your Tinker skill to stabilize dying creatures or repair constructs as if you were using the Medicine skill.
Additionally, you can craft basic gadgets during a rest. Choose one of the following:
* Flash Canister: As a bonus action, you can throw this gadget up to 30 feet. Each creature within 10 feet must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your Intelligence modifier) or be blinded until the start of their next turn. (Costs 1 Crafting Point)
* Smoke Vial: As a bonus action, create a 10-foot-radius lightly obscured smoke cloud lasting 1 minute or until dispersed. (Costs 1 Crafting Point)
You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). You regain all expended Crafting Points after a rest.

Quick Repair
2nd-level Artificer feature
Your expertise in crafting and mending mechanical devices allows you to quickly patch up constructs. During a short rest, you can repair a construct within 5 feet of you. When you use your Tinker skill to repair a creature, you restore hit points equal to your Artificer level + your Intelligence modifier.
Explosive Gadgets
2nd-level Artificer feature
You gain access to crafting explosive devices, usable in combat. When you rest, you can craft one of the following explosive gadgets:
* Fireburst Charge: Creates a small explosion of fire. The target must make a Dexterity save or take 2d6 fire damage (half on a successful save) within a 10-foot radius. (Costs 2 Crafting Points)
* Shrapnel Bomb: Explodes in a burst of jagged metal. Creatures within a 10-foot radius must make a Dexterity save or take 2d6 piercing damage (half on a successful save). (Costs 2 Crafting Points)
* Smoke Bomb: Creates a dense cloud of smoke in a 15-foot radius, causing all creatures in the area to be blinded for 1 minute or until they leave the cloud. (Costs 1 Crafting Point)
You can craft a number of gadgets equal to your Intelligence modifier per rest. You regain all expended Crafting Points after a rest.

Signature Invention
3rd-level Artificer feature 
You gain the ability to craft a Signature Invention, a unique creation that serves as your signature piece of engineering. You must choose one of the following options:
* Personal Suit of Armor: You craft a set of light armor that fits your form perfectly. It grants you a bonus to AC equal to your Intelligence modifier. This armor does not count toward your encumbrance for carrying items and you are always treated as proficient with this armor. At 10th level, this suit of armor you to add your Intelligence modifier to your Strength or Dexterity saving throws while wearing it.
* Mechanical Servant: You build a small, mechanical servant to assist you. It can perform simple tasks such as carrying objects, opening doors, and helping in combat. It has hit points equal to your Artificer level, an Armor Class of 12 + your Intelligence modifier, and it uses your Dexterity modifier for attack rolls. It can make a single attack per round, dealing 1d6 bludgeoning damage with its mechanical limbs. It does not speak but can understand simple commands. At 10th level, your Servant gains a bonus to its attack rolls and saving throws equal to your Intelligence modifier.
* "Cannon" Weapon: You craft a powerful, non-magical ranged weapon that you can wield like a crossbow or firearm. The weapon deals 1d6 damage of a type chosen by you (force, piercing, or thunder), has a range of 120 feet, and requires ammunition. You can use your Intelligence modifier for attack rolls with this weapon, and it can be reloaded as a bonus action. It does not function as a magical item but can be improved with later features. At 10th level, this increases to 1d10 damage.

Efficiency in Creation (Ex)
3rd-level Artificer feature
Your ability to craft items quickly and efficiently has improved. When you craft an item, you now require only half the materials (and time) to craft basic non-magical items, armor, weapons, or tools. Additionally, you can craft one non-magical item of uncommon rarity or lower during a rest by spending 1 Crafting point. This item must be an object you are proficient in crafting. The item crafted still requires the necessary crafting materials (now halved due to this feature).

Masterwork Invention (Ex)
4th-level Artificer feature
Your skills in crafting allow you to enhance your personal invention. Your choice of personal suit of armor, mechanical servant, or cannon weapon (from Signature Invention at Level 3) gains additional functionality. You can choose from the following enhancements based on your invention. You may select one enhancement when you first gain this feature, and you can choose an additional enhancement every 4 levels thereafter (at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 20th level). 
You can only have one of the enhancements active at a time. As an action, you may change the active enhancement to another you meet the requirements of.
Personal Suit of Armor:
You can now craft armor that provides an additional benefit. Choose one of the following enhancements:
* Reinforced Durability (Level 4): Your armor grants a +1 bonus to AC and is treated as one step more resistant to damage (e.g., it counts as resistance to a certain type of damage).
* Integrated Systems (Level 4): Your armor allows for increased movement. You can move up to 10 feet further per round.
* Tactical HUD (Level 8): Your armor integrates a visual enhancement, allowing you to see through heavy cover or obstacles, granting you the ability to clearly detect creatures hiding behind cover or objects in your path.
* Heavy Plating (Level 12): Your armor now grants a +2 bonus to AC and grants you resistance to a second damage type of your choice.
* Magnetic Field (Level 16): You can use your armor to create a magnetic field that can attract or repel small metal objects within 30 feet, allowing you to manipulate weapons or devices, or disarm an opponent. When you use this ability you make each creature in range make a Strength saving throw vs DC 8 + your Intelligence modifier. On a failure you can either disarm them if they wield metal weapons, or push them back 10 feet if they are wearing metal or composed of metal.
* Adaptive Shielding (Level 20): Your armor grants resistance to all damage types for 1 round after you take damage. You may use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of once per day).
Mechanical Servant:
Your mechanical servant becomes more versatile. Choose one of the following enhancements:
* Enhanced Mobility (Level 4): The servant can now move up to 10 feet faster than before, and its Dexterity modifier increases by +2.
* Weaponized Systems (Level 4): The servant can now wield a small weapon and make an attack once per turn.
* Built-in Shield (Level 8): The servant gains a +1 bonus to AC.
* Adaptive Frame (Level 12): Your servant can adapt to hostile environments, gaining resistance to one damage type of your choice (acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder).
* Self-Repairing Systems (Level 16): Your servant gains the ability to regain hit points equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of 1) as a bonus action once per combat.
* Rapid Assault Mode (Level 20): Your servant can take two actions on its turn, one of which can be a weapon attack.
Cannon Weapon:
Your "cannon" weapon now functions as a reliable, powerful tool. Choose one of the following enhancements:
* Power Boost (Level 4): Your cannon now does an additional 1d6 damage, damage matches the original type chosen for your Signature Invention.
* Grenade Launcher (Level 4): When you take the attack action with your Cannon, you may replace the attack with the usage of a bomb you have, using the Cannon�s range. 
* Rapid Fire (Level 4): You can attack twice instead of once whenever you take the�Attack�action on your turn with your Cannon, but both attacks must be made with the cannon and against a single target.
* Overcharge (Level 8): Your cannon can be charged up to unleash a devastating attack. You may spend your action to charge the cannon and make a single attack roll against a target. On a successful hit, the attack deals damage as if you had made all the attacks you could normally make during your turn. You can not move the turn you use this attack.
* Multi-target Mode (Level 12): Your cannon can now target two creatures within 20 feet of each other when you make a ranged attack with it. 
* Sustained Fire (Level 16): Must have chosen Rapid Fire. You can attack three times instead of once whenever you take the�Attack�action on your turn with your Cannon, but both attacks must be made with the cannon and against a single target.
* Devastating Barrage (Level 20): As an action your cannon fires an area-of-effect blast (15-foot radius) that deals 6d6 damage on a successful attack, and the target must make a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier) to take half damage. This feature can only be used once per long rest.
